## What Are Planetary Boundaries?

Planetary boundaries are ecological thresholds that, once crossed, risk destabilising Earthâ€™s systems. They include climate, biodiversity, freshwater, land use, ocean chemistry, and more. Staying within these limits is essential for a stable and livable planet.

## Historical Context

Developed by Earth system scientists, the framework shows that the capitalist model of endless growth has already pushed several boundaries past safe limits.

## Key Principles

- Finite ecological limits
- Interconnected environmental systems
- Irreversible tipping points
- Human activity as a destabilising force

## Modern Examples

- Exceeding safe levels of greenhouse gases
- Mass extinction and biodiversity collapse
- Ocean acidification
- Soil depletion and deforestation

## Relationship to Other Concepts

Planetary boundaries reinforce the eco-socialist argument that a system based on infinite growth contradicts ecological reality. A sustainable future requires democratic, post-extractive economic planning.
